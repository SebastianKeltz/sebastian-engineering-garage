Strongest Project

Electric Dirt Bike Conversion

Converted a dirt bike to electric drive using a 48V lithium battery, 2000W BLDC motor, motor controller, and throttle system.

This project required power delivery planning, controller troubleshooting, wiring decisions, mechanical fitment, current draw awareness, torque behavior, voltage drop testing, and safety checks under real load.

  • Integrated battery, controller, motor, throttle, wiring, and mounting hardware.
  • Tested electrical behavior under practical ride conditions instead of only bench assumptions.
  • Tracked issues around connectors, heat, controller response, voltage sag, and mechanical packaging.
  • Documented lessons learned so the next revision can be cleaner, safer, and easier to service.

Build Log

Documented progress beats perfect memory.

A simple build log helps recruiters see how I think: what I tried, what failed, how I tested it, and what I changed next.

Phase 01

Plan the system

Define the battery, controller, motor, wiring path, mounting constraints, safety concerns, and first test goals.

Phase 02

Assemble and test

Wire the system, verify connections, test throttle response, inspect heat, and check behavior under controlled conditions.

Phase 03

Troubleshoot under load

Investigate voltage sag, connector issues, controller behavior, mechanical alignment, and any intermittent failures.

Phase 04

Document the revision

Record the final configuration, lessons learned, test results, and changes planned for the next version.
