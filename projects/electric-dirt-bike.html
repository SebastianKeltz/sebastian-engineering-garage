Project Case Study

Electric Dirt Bike Conversion

Converting a small dirt bike platform into an electric mobility project using battery power, a BLDC motor, controller wiring, mechanical fitment, and testing.

View Resume Testing Notes

Overview

This project focuses on converting a small dirt bike platform into an electric vehicle system. The build combines battery placement, motor/controller wiring, throttle control, mechanical mounting, and real-world testing. The goal is not just to make it move, but to understand how the electrical and mechanical systems work together.

Engineering Goal

  • Build a functional electric dirt bike conversion.
  • Safely package the battery, controller, motor, and wiring.
  • Understand power flow from battery to controller to motor.
  • Test throttle response, wiring reliability, and mechanical fitment.
  • Improve the design through inspection, testing, and revisions.

Build Process

Project timeline by phase.

Phase 1

Planning and layout

Mapped where the battery, motor, controller, wiring, and throttle needed to fit.

Phase 2

Battery and motor fitment

Checked physical placement, clearance, mounting direction, and service access.

Phase 3

Controller and throttle wiring

Connected the control path and checked how the system responded before riding.

Phase 4

3D printed support parts

Used printed parts to support packaging, protection, and fitment problems.

Phase 5

First power checks

Verified connections and watched for obvious issues before higher-load testing.

Phase 6

Ride testing and revision

Inspected wiring, mounting, and system behavior after movement and vibration.

Testing and Troubleshooting

  • Checked connections before power-up.
  • Verified battery, controller, and motor layout.
  • Inspected wiring after movement or vibration.
  • Watched for heat, loose connections, and mechanical interference.
  • Revised mounting and wire routing after tests.

What I Learned

I learned how quickly electrical design becomes a packaging and safety problem once it is placed into a moving vehicle. The battery, controller, motor, wiring, and mounts all have to work together, and small layout decisions affect serviceability, reliability, and testing.
